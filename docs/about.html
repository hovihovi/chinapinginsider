About China Pinginsider

À propos de China Pinginsider

Chinese table tennis news, translated and curated. Because the best stories are written in Chinese.

L'actualité du tennis de table chinois, traduite et commentée. Parce que les meilleures histoires sont écrites en chinois.

What We Do

China Pinginsider is your window into Chinese table tennis, from the Chinese media's perspective. Our team monitors Chinese-language sports outlets including Sina Sports, Tencent News, and the official Chinese Table Tennis Association channels for the stories that matter. We translate them into English and French, adding context and background that international fans need to understand the full picture.

Chinese coverage of table tennis is extraordinarily rich. Post-match interviews go deeper. Coach press conferences reveal tactical thinking. Beat reporters have access that Western media rarely gets. Our job is to bridge that gap.

Why Pinginsider?

Ping Penguin. It is a silly pun that captures what we do: wade through the vast ocean of Chinese table tennis content and bring back the gems. Our mascot, a cheerful penguin with a paddle, represents our approach: serious about the sport, but never taking ourselves too seriously.

Sections

News. Match reports, squad announcements, tournament previews and results from the Chinese perspective.

Player Profiles. Deep dives on the athletes who dominate the world rankings, from their training habits to their career trajectories, as told by Chinese journalists.

Equipment. Coverage of Chinese equipment brands, blade and rubber reviews from Chinese sources, and behind-the-scenes looks at what the pros actually use.

Our Approach

Every article published here follows a simple process:

  1. Source. We identify noteworthy stories from Chinese media coverage.
  2. Translate and contextualise. Each piece is translated and enriched with background information that Western readers would not otherwise have.
  3. Review. Every article is reviewed before publication to ensure accuracy.

Languages

Articles are published in English and French. Use the EN/FR toggle at the top of each page to switch between versions.
