Zhang Jike Returns in Revolutionary "100-Point Battle"

Le retour de Zhang Jike : la « Bataille des 100 points »

Published June 29, 2026 · News
Publié le 29 juin 2026 · Actualités
  • Zhang Jike returned to competitive table tennis at 38 in a radical new format he created: the "100-point battle"
  • Final score: Team Ovtcharov defeated Team Zhang 100-88
  • 500 million+ total views on Douyin; 500,000+ concurrent viewers
  • Zhang lost his opening segment 12-15 to Cheng Jingqi and his captain duel to Ovtcharov 15-11
  • Controversy: Cao Wei withdrew over a last-minute format change

Zhang Jike, the "Tibetan Mastiff," is one of only four male players in history to complete the table tennis Grand Slam (World Cup, World Championships, Olympic singles gold). He did it faster than anyone: from his first World Cup win in November 2011 to his Olympic gold at London 2012, just 445 days elapsed. That record still stands.

Now 38 and years removed from WTT sanctioned events, Zhang stepped back onto the competitive stage on June 26 at the Hangzhou Lin'an Sports and Culture Expo Center. The vehicle for his return was a format he created himself: a 5v5 team "100-point battle" inspired by basketball streetball rules, livestreamed on Douyin.

The Rosters

Team Zhang (captain: Zhang Jike):

  • Zhang Jike (captain, shakehand, Grand Slam champion)
  • Yin Hang (world-class chopper, former national team)
  • Liu Jialiang (shakehand loop specialist, former national team)
  • Liang Jiayi (19-year-old female rising star)
  • 5th player slot reserved but never fielded during the match

Team Ovtcharov (captain: Dimitrij Ovtcharov):

  • Dimitrij Ovtcharov (captain, Germany, 2017 World Cup champion)
  • Cheng Jingqi (penhold standout, former national training partner of Ma Long)
  • Zhao Zihao (domestic player, emergency replacement after Cao Wei withdrawal)
  • Li Rensijia (European female player)

How It Works

No games, no time limit. First team to 100 cumulative points wins. The scoring team keeps serving, removing the usual safety valve of switching serve every two points. Water breaks only at 20, 40, 60, and 80 points. If tied at 99-99, a team must win by 2. Disciplines switch at each 15-point threshold: singles, singles, singles, singles, men's doubles, women's singles, mixed doubles. Both captains must remain on court for the entire match.

Match Progression

Segment 1 (0-15, singles): Cheng Jingqi defeated Zhang Jike 15-12.
Score: Team Ovtcharov 15, Team Zhang 12. Zhang started strong with reverse short serves and forehand loops to lead 2-0, but Cheng's sharp backhand angles and familiarity with Zhang's patterns turned the tide. Two missed backhand flips at 12-12 and a net cord on match point sealed Zhang's loss.

Segment 2 (15-30, singles): Zhao Zihao defeated Liu Jialiang 30-23.
Score: Team Ovtcharov 30, Team Zhang 23. Extended shakehand loop rallies. Liu's power kept him in points, but Zhao's faster close-to-table speed and superior short game gave him the edge.

Segment 3 (30-45, singles, captain vs captain): Ovtcharov defeated Zhang Jike 15-11.
Score: Team Ovtcharov 45, Team Zhang 34. The crowd erupted for the marquee matchup. Ovtcharov's backhand banana flip was as sharp as ever, pinning Zhang to his backhand corner. Zhang's forehand, once the most feared weapon in the sport, landed long on several high-percentage opportunities.

Segment 4 (45-60, singles): Cheng Jingqi defeated Yin Hang 60-52.
Score: Team Ovtcharov 60, Team Zhang 52. Yin's chopping style troubled Cheng early, drawing unexpected errors. But Cheng adjusted mid-segment, mixing high lobs with sudden smashes to force Yin into mistakes.

Segment 5 (60-75, men's doubles): Ovtcharov/Zhao Zihao defeated Zhang/Liu Jialiang 13-15.
Score: Team Ovtcharov 75, Team Zhang 65. Zhang's movement was noticeably slower. After stretching to save a ball, he paused to rub his lower back, waving off water. Zhang/Liu lost the doubles segment 13-15.

Segment 6 (75-90, women's singles): Li Rensijia defeated Liang Jiayi 11-15.
Score: Team Ovtcharov 90, Team Zhang 76. Liang brought aggressive energy, but her lack of high-level experience showed against Li's sharp angle changes. The lead grew to 14 points.

Segment 7 (90-100, mixed doubles): Zhang/Liang Jiayi won 12-10. Final score: Team Ovtcharov 100-88.
Score: Team Ovtcharov 100, Team Zhang 88. Before the final segment, Zhang pulled a 2012 London Olympic podium photo from his pocket, showing himself on the top step and Ovtcharov on the bronze step. Ovtcharov laughed and held it up to the crowd. Zhang shifted into high gear, serving fast long balls to catch Ovtcharov off guard, while Liang added sharp net play. They won the segment 12-10, but the early deficit was too large.

"It is always special to play Jike in front of his home fans. His forehand still has that same fire that made him so hard to beat back in our prime." Dimitrij Ovtcharov

Controversy: Cao Wei's Withdrawal

Former national team player Cao Wei withdrew on the day of the match after a dispute over format. He had agreed to participate on the condition of a singles match against Zhang Jike, but was informed upon arrival on June 24 that his slot had been changed to doubles. After failing to reach a resolution, he left Hangzhou.

What's Next

In a post-match interview, Zhang teased that he has already sent invitations to other retired stars for future editions, naming Timo Boll and Ma Lin as potential guests. The event drew over 500 million total views on Douyin, suggesting the format may have legs beyond this single exhibition.

Beyond the match itself, Zhang has been building a broader table tennis infrastructure. In May 2025, he opened a 4,000+ m² training facility in Hangzhou Lin'an with 36 ITTF certified tables. In July 2025, he launched the "Zhang Jike Cup", China's first nationwide amateur tournament named after an active player.

Zhang Jike, le « Dogue Tibétain », est l'un des quatre seuls joueurs masculins de l'histoire à avoir réalisé le Grand Chelem (Coupe du monde, Mondiaux, or olympique). Il l'a fait plus vite que quiconque : de sa première Coupe du monde en novembre 2011 à l'or olympique de Londres 2012, seulement 445 jours se sont écoulés. Ce record tient toujours.

À 38 ans, Zhang est remonté sur la scène compétitive le 26 juin au Hangzhou Lin'an Sports and Culture Expo Center. Le format qu'il a créé : une bataille par équipe 5c5 en 100 points inspirée du streetball, diffusée sur Douyin.

Les équipes

Team Zhang (capitaine : Zhang Jike) :

  • Zhang Jike (capitaine, Grand Chelem)
  • Yin Hang (spécialiste du chop, ancien international)
  • Liu Jialiang (spécialiste du top spin, ancien international)
  • Liang Jiayi (19 ans, espoir féminin)
  • 5e joueur réservé mais jamais entré en jeu pendant le match

Team Ovtcharov (capitaine : Dimitrij Ovtcharov) :

  • Dimitrij Ovtcharov (capitaine, Allemagne, champion du monde 2017)
  • Cheng Jingqi (porte-plume, ancien partenaire d'entraînement de Ma Long)
  • Zhao Zihao (joueur domestique, remplacement d'urgence après le forfait de Cao Wei)
  • Li Rensijia (joueuse européenne)

Déroulement du match

Segment 1 (0-15, simple) : Cheng Jingqi bat Zhang Jike 15-12. Score : Ovtcharov 15, Zhang 12. Zhang mène 2-0, mais Cheng renverse la situation par ses angles de revers et sa connaissance du jeu de Zhang.

Segment 2 (15-30, simple) : Zhao Zihao bat Liu Jialiang, score cumulé 30-23. Zhao impose sa vitesse en jeu court.

Segment 3 (30-45, simple, capitaine contre capitaine) : Ovtcharov bat Zhang Jike 15-11. Score : Ovtcharov 45, Zhang 34. Le duel tant attendu. Le revers d'Ovtcharov est chirurgical. Plusieurs top spins de Zhang finissent longs.

Segment 4 (45-60, simple) : Cheng Jingqi bat Yin Hang, score : Ovtcharov 60, Zhang 52. Yin gêne Cheng par ses chops, mais Cheng s'adapte en mixant lobs hauts et smashs.

Segment 5 (60-75, double messieurs) : Ovtcharov/Zhao Zihao bat Zhang/Liu Jialiang 13-15. Score : Ovtcharov 75, Zhang 65. Zhang ralentit, se touche le bas du dos.

Segment 6 (75-90, simple dames) : Li Rensijia bat Liang Jiayi 11-15. Score : Ovtcharov 90, Zhang 76. L'expérience parle, l'écart monte à 14 points.

Segment 7 (90-100, double mixte) : Zhang/Liang Jiayi gagne 12-10. Score final : Team Ovtcharov 100-88. Avant le dernier segment, Zhang sort de sa poche la photo du podium olympique de Londres 2012, lui sur la plus haute marche, Ovtcharov sur la troisième. Il la tend à son rival qui éclate de rire et la montre au public.

"C'est toujours spécial de jouer contre Jike devant son public. Son coup droit a toujours ce même feu qui le rendait si difficile à battre à notre prime." Dimitrij Ovtcharov

Et après ?

Zhang a annoncé en interview qu'il a déjà invité Timo Boll et Ma Lin pour les prochaines éditions. L'événement a cumulé plus de 500 millions de vues sur Douyin, suggérant que le format a de l'avenir.
